EmailJS Setup Guide for PDFextend

This guide will help you set up EmailJS to send feedback emails from your PDFextend application.

Note: EmailJS offers a free tier that allows you to send 200 emails per month, which should be sufficient for your feedback form.

Step 1: Create an EmailJS Account

  1. Go to https://www.emailjs.com/signup/
  2. Sign up for a free account
  3. Verify your email address

Step 2: Add an Email Service

  1. Log in to your EmailJS account
  2. Go to "Email Services" in the left sidebar
  3. Click "Add New Service"
  4. Select "Gmail" or any other email provider you prefer
  5. Follow the instructions to connect your email account
  6. Give your service a name (e.g., "PDFextend Feedback")
  7. Save the service
  8. Important: Note down your Service ID (e.g., "service_abc123")

Step 3: Create an Email Template

  1. Go to "Email Templates" in the left sidebar
  2. Click "Create New Template"
  3. Give your template a name (e.g., "Feedback Template")
  4. In the "To email" field, enter your email address: code.canogullari@gmail.com
  5. In the "Subject" field, enter: {{subject}}
  6. In the "Content" field, enter: {{{message}}}
  7. Save the template
  8. Important: Note down your Template ID (e.g., "template_xyz789")

Step 4: Get Your Public Key

  1. Go to "Account" in the left sidebar
  2. Look for "API Keys" section
  3. Important: Note down your Public Key (e.g., "user_abc123xyz789")

Step 5: Update Your Code

Open the app/components/FeedbackForm.tsx file and update the following lines:

// Initialize EmailJS
emailjs.init("YOUR_PUBLIC_KEY"); // Replace with your EmailJS public key

// ...

// Send email using EmailJS
await emailjs.send(
  'YOUR_SERVICE_ID', // Replace with your EmailJS service ID
  'YOUR_TEMPLATE_ID', // Replace with your EmailJS template ID
  templateParams
);

Replace:

  • YOUR_PUBLIC_KEY with your Public Key from Step 4
  • YOUR_SERVICE_ID with your Service ID from Step 2
  • YOUR_TEMPLATE_ID with your Template ID from Step 3
Security Note: It's safe to include these keys in your client-side code because EmailJS has built-in security measures to prevent abuse. Your EmailJS account is protected by domain restrictions and rate limiting.

That's it!

Your feedback form should now send emails to code.canogullari@gmail.com with a unique subject line for each submission and links to any attached images.
